What is Gaussian Splatting?

Gaussian Splatting represents a 3D scene as millions of tiny anisotropic Gaussian blobs. Each splat carries position, scale, rotation, opacity, and colour — producing photorealistic environments that can be explored freely from any angle.

The environments on this site are pregenerated offline and streamed to your browser. Use a mouse or touch to navigate, or put on a VR headset for a fully immersive walkthrough.

How It Works

  1. 1

    Capture

    Photograph the scene from many angles.

  2. 2

    Initialize

    Build a sparse point cloud via Structure-from-Motion.

  3. 3

    Optimize

    Train splat parameters to match the captured images.

  4. 4

    Render

    Rasterize splats in real time from any viewpoint.
